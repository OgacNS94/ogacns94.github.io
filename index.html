About Me

I am a PhD student at School of Computer and Communication Sciences, EPFL. I am working at the Parallel Systems Architecture Laboratory PARSA with Dr. Mirjana Stojilović and Prof. Babak Falsafi. My main area of research is security of remote FPGAs. I have a Computer Science Masters degree from the Faculty of Science and Engineering of the Sorbonne Université, for which I receieved a French government scholarship for international students. I obtained my Bachelor's degree in Electronics as Electrical Engineering from the University of Novi Sad. During my studies, I had multiple industry internships, the most notable one was at ARM France SAS in 2018, and at Elsys Eastern Europe in 2016. In 2018, I joined EPFL as an EDIC doctoral fellowship student. You can find the most recent version of my CV here.

Apart form my education and work, I am an AFOL (Adult Fan of LEGO), meaning that I am crazy about LEGOs and I love creating my own LEGO builds. You can find home of my cool builds here. I also love choir singing, and I have sung in many different choirs ever since I joined high school. You can find a couple of cool videos of me and my boys here.
